# Step 2: Competitive Teardown

Reference the master context document for all locked decisions and success criteria.
Reference the Step 1 output for target users, use cases, and quality bars.

---

## Your task

Analyze the five main competitors and produce a clear win matrix showing exactly where this product beats them.

---

## Deliver exactly these sections

### 1. Product-by-product analysis

Analyze each of these five products:
1. Yahoo Fantasy
2. ESPN Fantasy
3. Sleeper
4. Fantrax
5. Underdog Fantasy (as a UX/design reference, not a format comp)

For each product, provide:
- **Strengths** (2 to 4, be specific about what they nail)
- **Weaknesses** (2 to 4, be specific about what frustrates users)
- **UX failures** (1 to 3 specific flows that are confusing or slow)
- **What to steal** (1 to 2 patterns worth copying)
- **What to avoid** (1 to 2 patterns to explicitly reject)

### 2. Win matrix

A table with these columns:
- Product dimension (e.g., "Lineup editing speed," "Draft room clarity," "Mobile matchup view")
- Yahoo
- ESPN
- Sleeper
- Fantrax
- This product

Rate each: Strong / Adequate / Weak. For "This product," describe the specific design choice that wins.

Include at least 12 product dimensions. Prioritize the ones that matter most to the target users from Step 1.

### 3. Competitive positioning statement

One paragraph. Where does this product sit in the market? What is the one-sentence pitch that makes it obvious why someone would switch?

---

## Format

Use the table format for the win matrix. Short paragraphs for analysis. Keep total output under 3,000 words.

---

- Product strategy (already done in Step 1)
- Information architecture (that's Step 3)
