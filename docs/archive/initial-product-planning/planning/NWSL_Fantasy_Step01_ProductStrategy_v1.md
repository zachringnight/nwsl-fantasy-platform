# Step 1: Product Strategy Framing

Reference the master context document for all locked decisions, constraints, and priority tiers.

---

## Your task

Produce a product strategy document that frames the entire build. This is the foundation every subsequent step builds on.

---

## Deliver exactly these sections

### 1. Target users (define 3 to 4 segments)
For each segment:
- Who they are
- What they want from fantasy sports
- Their experience level with fantasy and with NWSL
- What would make them choose this product over doing nothing

### 2. Primary use cases (top 5 to 7)
Ranked by frequency and importance. For each:
- The user action
- When and where it happens
- What success looks like

### 3. Why existing fantasy products are weak for NWSL
Be specific. Name the product and the failure. Do not generalize.

### 4. What "best in class" means in practice
Define 5 to 7 concrete quality bars this product must hit. Make them measurable or at least observable. "Better UX" is not a quality bar. "Lineup set in under 3 taps from league home" is.

### 5. The NWSL-specific product wedge
What makes an NWSL fantasy product fundamentally different from NFL/NBA fantasy? What design decisions follow from that difference?

### 6. MVP vs Phase 2 vs Phase 3 scope
Using the priority tiers from the master context, produce a clear scope table:
- MVP (what ships for season launch)
- Phase 2 (what ships mid-season or early next season)
- Phase 3 (future roadmap)

For each item, one sentence on why it's in that phase.

---

## Format

Use headers and short paragraphs. No bullet-point walls. Tables where they clarify scope or comparisons. Keep total output under 3,000 words.

---

## Do not include
- Competitive teardown (that's Step 2)
- Technical architecture (that's Step 7)
- Screen specs (that's Step 8)
- Implementation details
